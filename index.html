Instructions for this experiment
For each highlighted sentence, identify what emotion the sentence may try to create and what strategy it uses. The rating popup appears only after you press Next.
1. Label the sentence, not the whole email Use only the highlighted sentence. Do not use later sentences to decide the labels for the current one.
2. Choose both emotion and strategy when present Emotional labels describe the reaction being targeted. Strategy labels describe what the sentence is doing.
3. Press Next to rate selected labels After choosing labels, press Next. A popup will ask you to rate each selected label from 1 to 5.
4. Use neutral carefully Neutral means no clear emotional trigger. It cannot be combined with other emotional labels, but strategy labels can still be selected.
5. Use unclear only when needed Select unclear/hard to label only when the sentence is difficult to understand or cannot be labeled confidently.
6. Do not judge whether the email is real or fake The task is to label emotional triggers and strategies, not to decide whether the email is genuine or suspicious.
